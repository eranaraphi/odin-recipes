Sinigang Recipe



Pork Sinigang

Pork Sinigang with Taro is a Filipino pork soup cooked in a sour broth. Sinigang is my comfort food, and one of the first few dishes that I tried to make way back when I was starting to learn how to cook.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly sliced into cubes
  • 2 to 3 cups kangkong leaves
  • 3 pieces long green chili pepper
  • 2 pieces tomato quartered
  • 1 cup sliced daikon radish
  • 10 to 12 pieces snake beans cut in 2 inch length pieces
  • 1 piece onion quartered
  • 15 pieces okra
  • 2 pieces taro peeled and cut in half
  • 8 cups water
  • 2 pack Knorr Sinigang sa Samploc Mix Original 20 gram pack
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

Instructions

  1. Pour water into cooking pot. Let boil
  2. Add onion and tomata. boil for 5 to 8 minutes.
  3. Put the pork in the cooking pot. Boil for 25 minutes
  4. Add the taro. Cover and continue to boil for 30 minutes
  5. Add Knorr Sinigang sa Sampaloc mix, fish sauce, and ground black pepper; stir.
  6. Put the daikon radish in. Cook for 3 to 5 minutes.
  7. Add the chili peppers, okra and snake beans. Cook for 7 minutes.
  8. Add the kangkong. Cover the cooking pot and turn off the heat. Let it stay covered for 5 minutes.
  9. Transfer to a serving bowl. Serve with small bowl with fish sauce and spicy chili.
  10. Share and enjoy
